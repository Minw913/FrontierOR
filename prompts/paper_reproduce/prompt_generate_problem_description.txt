## Style and Format Rules (MANDATORY — follow every rule exactly)

(1) Apart from the "# Problem Description" header, write the entire output as continuous prose paragraphs. Do NOT use LaTeX, equations, equation blocks, bullet points, numbered lists, additional section headers, markdown formatting, or any other structural markup.

(2) Describe mathematical relationships verbally. The text must encode the same information as the formulas, but without symbols.

(3) Do NOT use explicit modeling-language jargon such as "decision variable," "constraint," "objective function," "feasibility," "binary variable," "continuous variable," "nonnegativity," or "for modeling purposes." Describe things in operational/business terms: "the company must choose…," "each machine may serve at most…," "the goal is to minimize…"

(4) Omit common-sense mathematical properties (nonnegativity of physical quantities, integrality of yes/no decisions) UNLESS they encode a substantive modeling requirement that a reader could not infer from the business context alone.

(5) Do NOT restate the same rule more than once. Each sentence should convey new information.

(6) Do NOT include examples, worked instances, intuitive explanations, motivational storytelling, algorithmic hints, or commentary on problem difficulty.
